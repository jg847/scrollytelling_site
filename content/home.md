## Welcome to the surface

Welcome to the tour. Ready to take a dive into the ocean?

Below your feet, the water doesn't just get deeper — it changes. Light fades. Pressure builds. Familiar fish give way to creatures that make their own light, and then to creatures that have never seen any.

We'll descend through five zones in one continuous scroll.

![bg 50% 36% | Sunlit surface of the vast](/images/ocean.jpg)

## The descent begins in light

The first few hundred meters still feel familiar. Sunlight scatters green-blue through the water. Fish move in schools. Life is busy, fast, and visible. But that comfort is thin. Most of the ocean lies below it, and almost every meter downward changes the rules.

## The five layers

```stat-grid
<200m | The Euphotic Zone
200m-1,000m | The Dysphotic Zone
1,000m-4,000m | The Aphotic Zone
4,000m-6,000m | The Abyssal Zone
> 6,000m | The Hadal Zone
```

## What changes as you sink

Light fades first. Then temperature drops. Pressure rises constantly. Animals get slower, stranger, and more specialized. By the time you reach the trenches, the creatures around you are surviving conditions that would crush a submarine.
